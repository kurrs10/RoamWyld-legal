Why Entry Requirements Are Complicated

Entry requirements are not universal — they depend entirely on your passport. A US citizen and a UK citizen traveling together to Japan face the same rules, but those same two travelers heading to Brazil may face completely different visa requirements. And rules change: countries update visa policies, add electronic travel authorizations, and change passport validity minimums with little notice.

The consequences of getting entry requirements wrong are severe. Airlines can deny boarding if your passport doesn't meet the destination's minimum validity requirements. Border officers can deny entry if you don't have the right documents. And if something goes wrong mid-trip — transiting through a country you didn't plan to enter deeply — being unable to look up requirements quickly matters.

Always verify entry requirements with official government sources before travel. Requirements change and Roam Wyld's data, while regularly updated, should be treated as a reference and starting point — not a legal guarantee. For the most current information, check your government's official travel advisory (travel.state.gov for US travelers, gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice for UK travelers).

What Entry Requirements Cover

Entry requirements typically include several distinct categories, all of which matter for international travel:

  • Visa requirements: Do you need a visa, and if so, what type? Tourist visa, eVisa, visa on arrival, or visa-free entry?
  • Passport validity: Most countries require your passport to be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay. Some require 3 months. A few countries require only that it's valid for the duration of your stay.
  • Maximum stay: Even without a visa, most countries limit how long you can stay on a single entry as a tourist (commonly 30, 60, or 90 days).
  • eTA / travel authorization: Some countries (Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and soon the EU with ETIAS) require an electronic travel authorization even for visa-free entry. These are usually quick and inexpensive but easy to forget.
  • Blank pages: Many countries require a minimum number of blank pages in your passport for stamps.
  • Health requirements: Some countries require vaccinations (yellow fever is the most common requirement, especially in parts of Africa and South America).

Entry Requirements by Passport

Roam Wyld supports entry requirements for five major passport nationalities, with rules specific to each:

US Passport

Visa-free or on-arrival access to 186 countries. Strong for Europe, Asia-Pacific, and most of the Americas. Requires eTA for Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.

UK Passport

Visa-free or on-arrival access to 188 countries. Post-Brexit, UK citizens now need to track Schengen days like other non-EU nationals (90/180 days).

Australian Passport

Visa-free or on-arrival access to 185 countries. Strong across Asia-Pacific. US entry requires ESTA. EU entry will require ETIAS from late 2026.

Canadian Passport

Visa-free or on-arrival access to 185 countries. Similar profile to Australian passport. Requires eTA for flights to or connecting through Canada (even for Canadians).

EU Passport

Full freedom of movement within the EU. Visa-free access to 190+ countries. Strongest travel document in the world by most rankings.

The Problem With Most Travel Apps

Most travel apps that include entry requirements have two critical problems: they require an internet connection to display the information, and they show generic rules that aren't filtered to your specific passport.

Generic rules are misleading. "Japan: 90 days visa-free" is true for US and UK passport holders but not for all nationalities. An app that shows you the most common rule without knowing your passport can give you false confidence.

And the internet requirement is a real problem at the exact moment you most need this information: clearing customs in a foreign country, often without a working SIM card, sometimes in an area with no WiFi.

Coming in Late 2026: ETIAS

ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System) is the EU's pre-travel registration system, similar to the US ESTA or Australia's ETA. It is expected to launch in late 2026 and will apply to travelers from 59 visa-exempt nationalities — including the US, UK, Australia, and Canada — entering the Schengen Area.

ETIAS is not a visa. It is a €7 online registration that takes a few minutes, is valid for 3 years, and must be obtained before you board. It does not change the 90/180-day Schengen limit — it is an additional pre-travel step.

Roam Wyld will add ETIAS reminders and requirements to entry requirement data when the system launches.
