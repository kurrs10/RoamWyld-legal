Your trip, entry requirements, emergency contacts, and currency data — all on your phone, no signal needed.

Why Offline Matters for International Travel

Most travel apps work beautifully at home, on your couch, with fast WiFi. They fall apart exactly when you need them most: at a border crossing, on a cruise ship between ports, in a foreign airport without a local SIM, or anywhere your international roaming plan runs out of data.

This is especially true for the information that matters most when you're traveling internationally — visa rules, emergency numbers, entry requirements. These are the things you need to look up at the moment of arrival, not before you leave home.

Real scenario: You land in Tokyo at 11pm. Your US SIM has no roaming plan. The airport WiFi requires a Japanese phone number to activate. You need to know whether you need to fill out a customs declaration card and where the nearest pharmacy opens at 8am. A travel app that requires internet is useless. Roam Wyld works.

What Works Offline in Roam Wyld

Roam Wyld caches all of this locally on your device when you first open each section. No internet needed to access it later:

Your Itinerary

Full trip timeline, all bookings (flights, hotels, activities), confirmation numbers, and dates.

Entry Requirements

Visa rules, passport validity requirements, and entry checklists for every country on your trip — filtered to your passport nationality.

Emergency Contacts

Police, ambulance, fire, and tourist police numbers for every country on your trip. Always one tap away.

Currency Data

Exchange rates cached at last sync. See what your home currency is worth in local currency without an internet connection.

Schengen Tracker

Your Schengen day calculations work fully offline — no need for internet at a border crossing.

What Requires Internet

A small number of features require an active connection because they depend on live data:

Gmail Import

Scanning your inbox for booking confirmations requires a connection. Do this at home before you travel.

Booking Validation

Checking your flight number against live airline data requires internet. Validate before your trip.

Live Exchange Rates

Refreshing currency rates to today's rate requires a connection. Cached rates are always available offline.

The design principle is simple: anything you might need in-destination — where connectivity is uncertain — is available offline. Anything that requires live data can be done at home or in your hotel with WiFi before you head out.

How Offline Caching Works

Roam Wyld caches your trip data directly on your device. When you view your itinerary, entry requirements, or any other section of the app, that data is written to local storage automatically. You do not need to do anything special to enable offline mode — it is on by default.

When you reconnect, the app refreshes data that may have updated. Changes require an active connection to save — so if you are planning and editing your trip in the app, make sure to do that before going fully offline.

Offline for Specific Travel Scenarios

Cruises

Cruise ship WiFi is notoriously expensive and unreliable. Roam Wyld's offline architecture was specifically designed with this in mind — your port entry requirements, emergency contacts, and currency data for every stop are cached before you board. The cruise itself is disconnected territory; Roam Wyld works the whole time.

Remote Destinations

Trekking in Nepal, traveling through rural Southeast Asia, or visiting smaller islands often means no data coverage. Roam Wyld treats connectivity as optional, not required.

Border Crossings

Land border crossings between countries are where internet coverage is least reliable and entry requirement information is most critical. Roam Wyld's offline entry requirements are specifically valuable here — you can check visa rules, passport validity requirements, and Schengen day counts without needing a signal.

Layovers and Transits

Even short transits through a country can have entry requirements — some countries require transit visas even if you're not leaving the airport. Roam Wyld includes transit requirements as part of entry information for destinations on your trip.
